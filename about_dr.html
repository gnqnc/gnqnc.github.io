ABOUT DR. GALINA NIKOLSKAYA

Dr. Galina Nikolskaya is a top ranked neurologist in an active clinical practice, recognized as a Top 1 Neurologist in the Orange Country Region during a Spring 2017 HealthTap Best Doctor competition and Americaâ€™s Top Physician 2017 by Consumers Research Council of America.

Residency

Dr. Nikolskaya completed her residency in neurology at a highly reputable and rigorous residency program at the University of California San Diego.

Fellowship

Dr. Nikolskaya completed an ACGME accredited clinical neurophysiology fellowship at Harbor-UCLA. During the fellowship training, she became skilled in electrodiagnostics including electroencephalography (EEG), electromyography (EMG), nerve conduction studies (NCS). During her fellowship, she acquired additional expertise in neuromuscular disorders, epilepsy, vagal nerve stimulator programming, and botulinum toxin treatment of various neurological conditions including chronic migraines, cervical dystonia, arm or leg dystonia, blepharospasm, hemifacial spasm.

Clinical Interests

Besides neuromuscular disorders, clinical neurophysiology and botulinum toxin treatments, Dr. Nikolskaya has an additional interest and experience in the various headache disorders and traumatic brain injuries. Her clinical skills of managing migraines and other headache disorders were further enhanced while rotating at the Headache Center of South California. Dr. Nikolskaya also has expertise in movement disorders having spent extra time learning from accomplished experts in the field. She has extensive clinical experience with movement disorders such as Parkinson's disease, essential tremor, tardive dyskinesia and restless leg syndrome and performs Botox injections for treatment of dystonia and spasticity.

Publications

Dr. Nikolskaya has published articles including co-authoring a review article on Glossopharyngeal neuralgia (cranial nerve number 9) in a Current Pain Headache Report journal and an article on FDG-PET Regional Glucose Abnormalities in White Matter in Civilian Impact and Military Blast-Related Mild Traumatic Brain Injury in the Journal of Biological Psychiatry.

Professional Certifications

She is a diplomate of an American Academy of Neurology and Psychiatry in Neurology and a diplomate of an American Board of Medical Specialties in Clinical Neurophysiology. She also holds a certification as a Qualified Medical Evaluator in a Division of Workers Compensation in California.

Academic Affiliation

Dr. Nikolskaya serves as an associate staff neurologist at Harbor - UCLA Medical Center affiliated with the David Geffen School of Medicine at UCLA, where she teaches neurology to medical students and residents.

Professional Society Membership

Dr. Nikolskaya is an active member of an American Academy of Neurology and enjoys staying up to date with all the current developments in the field.
